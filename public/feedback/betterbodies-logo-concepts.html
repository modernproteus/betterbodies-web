BetterBodies TX Rebrand

Logo concept directions for a stronger, more local brand.

These are rough creative territories, not final artwork. The goal is to find the direction that feels most like BetterBodies: Austin-rooted, hands-on, practical, strong, and ready for CPR, BLS, crisis response, self-defense, and future training services.

Review concepts Leave feedback
How to review this page Don’t worry about exact colors, fonts, or tiny details yet. Focus on the overall feeling: which direction feels most authentic, memorable, local, trustworthy, and flexible enough for all BetterBodies services?

Creative directions

Each option can be refined into a clean logo system with a primary mark, website header version, badge version, and service-specific variants.

The Austin Signal

Stay Ready, Austin.

A heartbeat line meets a simplified Austin skyline/Texas signal. This is the most local-facing direction.

  • Best for Austin pride and community connection
  • Strong fit for CPR/BLS without feeling too clinical
  • Can become a clean badge, shirt graphic, or homepage hero mark

The Preparedness Crest

CPR • BLS • Crisis • Defense

A structured shield crest that represents BetterBodies as a complete preparedness training brand.

  • Best for multi-service positioning
  • Feels official, strong, and certification-friendly
  • Great for patches, certificates, course materials, and apparel

The Coach Mark

Train With the Man With the Plan.

A founder-led/instructor-led direction built around the idea that Sheldon’s presence is the trust signal.

  • Best if Sheldon should be central to the brand
  • Feels personal, hands-on, and real
  • Works well with real photography and instructor storytelling

The Human Pulse

Confidence Through Training.

A modern, cleaner mark using a human shape and pulse line. This is the least aggressive and most professional option.

  • Best for healthcare workers, schools, and businesses
  • Simple, calm, and flexible
  • Can support CPR, BLS, and wellness-adjacent services

The Street-Safe Badge

Stay Ready.

A tougher patch-style direction with community safety energy. More street-smart than clinical.

  • Best for apparel, community events, and self-defense tie-ins
  • Bold, memorable, and easy to recognize
  • Could be softened for the main website logo

The Responder Mark

Ready Hands. Steady Hearts.

A human/protective symbol focused on action: being ready to help when something happens.

  • Best for an emotional, mission-driven brand
  • Connects CPR, crisis response, and community care
  • Feels less like a class provider and more like a readiness movement
